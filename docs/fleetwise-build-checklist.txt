1 · Product scope & principles
FR-1.1P0 Manage every asset on a farm in one system: vehicles, implements and equipment.
FR-1.2P0 Every data-capture action (service, fuel, fault) completable in under 30 seconds on mobile.
FR-1.3P0 Full functionality offline; nothing critical requires a live connection.
FR-1.4P1 Every record is auditable: who created/changed it, when, and from where.
FR-1.5P0 Multi-farm / multi-site support under one account, with data isolation per site.

2 · User roles & permissions
FR-2.1P0 Role-based access control with at least: Owner, Manager, Operator/Driver, Workshop/Mechanic,
External Contractor.
FR-2.2P0 Unlimited users per account, regardless of plan.
FR-2.3P1 Per-role visibility: operators see only their assigned assets; contractors see only assigned jobs.
FR-2.4P1 Invite/deactivate users; enforce least-privilege by default.
FR-2.5P2 Custom roles with granular permission toggles.
3 · Asset register
FR-3.1P0 Create/edit/archive assets of any type (tractor, bakkie, truck, implement, pump,
generator, other).
FR-3.2P0 Per-asset fields: make, model, year, registration, VIN/serial, purchase date & price, finance
details, photos.
FR-3.3P0 Live status per asset: In use, Available, In workshop, Out of service.
FR-3.4P1 Group/filter by type, location, cost centre, department.
FR-3.5P0 Current engine-hour and odometer reading per asset, updated on each relevant log.
FR-3.6P1 Assign a default operator/driver to each asset.
FR-3.7P1 Bulk import assets (CSV/Excel) during onboarding.
4 · Maintenance & servicing
FR-4.1P0 Complete, dated service/maintenance history per machine (fuel, parts, labour,
breakages, repairs).
FR-4.2P0 Schedule services by engine hours, kilometres AND calendar time, whichever is due first.
FR-4.3P0 Recurring service intervals per machine (e.g. every 250 engine hours).
FR-4.4P0 Due / due-soon / overdue states computed automatically from latest readings.
FR-4.5P1 Attach photos and notes to every service record.
FR-4.6P1 Job cards: plan a service, mark in-progress, complete, and record who did it.
FR-4.7P2 Warranty period tracking per machine with expiry reminders (links to §13).
5 · Service kits & parts
FR-5.1P0 Pre-loaded service kit per machine: exact engine oil, gearbox oil and filter part numbers.
FR-5.2P1 Parts catalogue with part number, description, supplier and typical cost.
FR-5.3P1 Parts consumed on a service auto-append to that machine's cost and history.
FR-5.4P2 Optional parts inventory/stock levels with low-stock alerts.

6 · Fuel & consumables
FR-6.1P0 Log fuel entries per asset (litres, cost, date, engine hours/odo, operator).
FR-6.2P0 Compute consumption (L/hr or L/100km) and trend per asset.
FR-6.3P0 Anomaly detection: flag fuel entries that deviate from the asset's baseline (possible leak/theft).
FR-6.4P2 Import fuel-card transactions and reconcile against logged entries (links to §17).
7 · Fault & issue reporting
FR-7.1P0 Operators log a fault against an asset in seconds, with description, severity and photos.
FR-7.2P0 Each fault captures asset, reporter, timestamp and (if available) location.
FR-7.3P1 Fault lifecycle: Open →  Acknowledged →  In progress →  Resolved, with assignee.
FR-7.4P1 A fault can be converted into a job card / service (links to §4).
FR-7.5P1 Reporting a fault can flip asset status to Out of service (links to §3).
8 · Contractor & mechanic portal
FR-8.1P0 External mechanics get scoped access to log work only on assigned assets/jobs.
FR-8.2P0 Upload quotes, invoices and job photos against an asset.
FR-8.3P1 Update job status; owner/manager notified on change (links to §14).
FR-8.4P1 Uploaded invoice amounts flow into that asset's cost record (links to §10).
9 · QR codes & field capture
FR-9.1P0 Generate a unique QR code per asset; printable as a weatherproof sticker.
FR-9.2P0 Scanning a QR opens that asset and offers quick actions: log service, log fuel, report fault.
FR-9.3P1 Scan-to-log works offline and queues for sync (links to §15).
FR-9.4P2 Re-issue/replace a QR code if a sticker is damaged, preserving asset link.
10 · Costs & true cost of ownership
FR-10.1P0 Every cost (fuel, parts, labour, external invoice, finance/interest) attributed to its asset.
FR-10.2P0 True cost of ownership per asset (purchase + finance + fuel + maintenance + repairs).
FR-10.3P0 Cost per engine hour and per kilometre, computed and displayed.
FR-10.4P1 Budgets per asset/category with budget-vs-actual tracking.
FR-10.5P2 Repair-vs-replace indicator when lifetime cost crosses a threshold of asset value.

FR-11.2P0 "Breaks most often" analysis: parts/machines with the highest repeat repairs.
FR-11.3P1 Selectable period ranges and per-site/per-group filtering.

FR-13.2P1 AARTO fine workflow: capture a fine, identify driver from usage log, track nomination deadline.
FR-13.3P1 Licence/renewal tracking with reminders.
FR-13.4P1 Export-audit pack for GLOBALG.A.P. / SIZA; vehicle-sale and warranty-claim document packs.
14 · Notifications & reminders
FR-14.1P0 Service-due, overdue and licence-expiry reminders via in-app, push and WhatsApp.
FR-14.2P1 Notify relevant roles on fault reported, job status change, and fuel anomaly.
FR-14.3P2 Per-user notification preferences and quiet hours.

FR-15.3P0 Deterministic conflict resolution (no silent data loss; last-writer + audit trail).
FR-15.4P1 Offline media (photos) cached and uploaded on reconnect.

20 · Feature interaction map
The system's value comes from how modules feed each other. Each of these data flows must exist end to end:
A fuel/service log updates the asset's engine hours/odometer → recomputes service-due state →  may fire
a reminder.
A QR scan or voice command or WhatsApp message all create the same underlying log records.
A fault report can set asset status Out of service, spawn a job card, and notify the manager.
A contractor invoice upload adds to the asset's cost record and true cost of ownership.
Every cost event feeds the dashboard metrics and cost-per-hour/km calculations.
Driver-to-vehicle usage logs power both accountability and AARTO nomination.
Any capture offline queues and later syncs, after which all dependent metrics recompute.

22 · Non-functional requirements
NFR-1P0 Performance: key screens interactive < 2s on 3G / low-end Android.
NFR-2P0 Security: encrypted in transit & at rest; hashed credentials; per-tenant data isolation.
NFR-3P0 POPIA compliance for personal/driver data; documented retention & deletion.
NFR-4P1 Reliability: automated backups, documented restore, ≥  99.5% uptime target.
NFR-5P1 Accessibility: legible in sunlight/high-contrast, large tap targets, screen-reader labels.
NFR-6P1 Observability: error logging, sync-failure alerts, usage analytics.
NFR-7P1 Scalability: thousands of assets per org without degradation.
23 · Metrics & stats to compute
The system must be able to derive and display all of the following:
• Machines due / overdue for service
• Total maintenance spend (period)
• Number of assets tracked
• Cost by machine (ranked)
• Parts/machines that break most often
• Total accumulated cost per asset (TCO)
• Cost per engine hour
• Cost per kilometre
• Fuel consumption (L/hr, L/100km) & trend
• Fuel anomalies flagged
• Downtime per asset
• Open vs resolved faults
• Budget vs actual
• Utilisation (hours used vs idle)
• Warranty/licence expiries upcoming
• AARTO nominations pending & deadlines
24 · Production readiness gate
